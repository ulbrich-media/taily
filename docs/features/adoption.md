# Adoption

An adoption is the complete process of finding and confirming a new owner for an animal. It begins when someone applies to adopt and ends when the animal has been successfully handed over — or when the process is canceled.

## General Status

Every adoption has a general status that gives the mediator a quick overview:

- **Pending**: The application has arrived but the mediator hasn't started working on it yet.
- **In progress**: The mediator is actively working through the adoption process.
- **Canceled**: The adoption was stopped at some point. A reason is always documented.
- **Done**: The animal has been handed over to the new owner. The adoption is complete.

## Process Steps

An adoption in progress can go through several steps. These steps are not enforced in a fixed order — the mediator decides which steps are needed and when. Steps can also be skipped entirely when they are not necessary for a particular adoption.

The typical sequence is:

1. **Application** — Someone has expressed interest and their basic information is collected. The mediator can add notes to document their assessment of the application.
2. **Pre-inspection** — A visit or video call is conducted to verify that the applicant's home is actually suitable for the animal. See [pre-inspection.md](./pre-inspection.md) for details.
3. **Contract** — A Schutzvertrag is signed by both the adopter and the mediator, defining rights and responsibilities. See [contract.md](./contract.md) for details.
4. **Transport** — The animal is transported to the adopter or a staging location. See [transport.md](./transport.md) for details.
5. **Handover** — The animal is physically handed over to the adopter. This marks the adoption as done.

## Cancellation

An adoption can be canceled at any point, regardless of which steps have been completed. The mediator documents a reason for the cancellation, which is kept separate from general internal notes to make it easy to understand why the adoption ended.

## Follow-up Inspection

After a successful adoption, a follow-up inspection may be conducted some time later to make sure the animal is thriving in its new home. This step does not change the adoption status — if something is found to be wrong, any follow-up action happens outside the normal adoption process.

## Additional Features

- All adoptions for a specific person or animal can be viewed directly from their respective pages.
- Multiple adoptions can exist for the same animal or person at different points in time.
